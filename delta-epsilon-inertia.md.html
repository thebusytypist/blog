Just find one application of the identity $\epsilon_{ijk} \epsilon_{lsk} =\delta_{il}\delta_{js} - \delta_{is}\delta_{jl}$. It is useful when we want to transform the inertia $\V{r}^* \V{r}^{*T}$ from the rest frame ($\hat{\V{r}}^* \hat{\V{r}}^{*T}$),where $\V{r}$ is the offset vector from the center of mass. $$ \begin{align*} \V{r}^* \V{r}^{*T} &= \epsilon_{ijk} r_j \epsilon_{lsk} r_s \\ &= r_j r_s \gp{\color{red}{\delta_{il}\delta_{js} - \delta_{is}\delta_{jl}}} \\ &= r_s r_s - r_l r_i \\ &= \V{r} \cdot \V{r} \V{\delta} - \V{r} \V{r}^T \end{align*} $$ Transform of inertia: $$ \begin{align*} \V{r}^* \V{r}^{*T} &= (\V{R} \hat{\V{r}})^*(\V{R} \hat{\V{r}})^{*T} \\ &= (\hat{\V{r}}^T \V{R}^T \V{R} \hat{\V{r}}) \V{\delta} - (\V{R} \hat{\V{r}} \hat{\V{r}} \V{R}^T) \\ &= \V{R} (\hat{\V{r}}^* \hat{\V{r}}^{*T}) \V{R}^T \end{align*} $$
