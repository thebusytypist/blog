$\newcommand{\zz}{\mathbf{z}}$ $\newcommand{\ttau}{\boldsymbol{\tau}}$ $\newcommand{\eye}{\boldsymbol{\delta}}$ Rotate the rigid body $\phi$ radians around unit axis $\nn$ through reference position $\xx$. $$ \begin{align} \QQ &= \QQ(\nn, \phi) \\ \xx'_i &= \xx + \QQ \rr_i \end{align} $$ Apply Rodrigues' rotation formula: $$ \begin{align} \QQ &= \eye + \sin{\phi} \cp{\nn} + (1-\cos{\phi})\cp{\nn}\cp{\nn} \\ \frac{\partial \QQ}{\partial \phi} &= \cos{\phi} \cp{\nn} + \sin(\phi) \cp{\nn}\cp{\nn} = \cp{\nn} \QQ \\ \frac{\partial \xx'_i}{\partial \phi} &= \cp{\nn} \QQ \rr_i = \nn \times (\QQ \rr_i) = -\QQ \rr_i \times \nn \end{align} $$ The kinetic energy $\KK$ should conserve for any $\phi$. $$ \begin{align} \KK &= \frac{1}{2} \sum_i m_i \gp{\frac{d \xx'_i}{d t}}^2 \\ \frac{d \KK}{d \phi} &= \sum_i m_i \gp{\frac{d \xx'_i}{d t}}^T \frac{\partial}{\partial \phi} \frac{d \xx'_i}{d t} = 0 \\ &= \sum_i m_i \frac{d}{dt} \gp{{\xx'}_i^T \frac{\partial \xx'_i}{\partial \phi}} \\ &= \sum_i m_i \frac{d}{dt} \gp{\gp{\xx + \QQ \rr_i}^T \frac{\partial \xx'_i}{\partial \phi}} \\ &= \sum_i m_i \frac{d}{dt} \gp{\gp{\QQ (\xx + \rr_i)}^T \frac{\partial \xx'_i}{\partial \phi}} = \sum_i m_i \frac{d}{dt} \gp{\gp{\xx + \rr_i}^T \QQ^T \frac{\partial \xx'_i}{\partial \phi}} \\ & = \sum_i \frac{d}{dt} \gp{(m_i \vv_i)^T \QQ^T \frac{\partial \xx'_i}{\partial \phi}} \quad (\frac{d}{dt} m_i \vv_i = \mathbf{0}) \\ &= -\frac{d}{dt} \sum_i \gp{ \gp{m_i \vv_i}^T \QQ^T \gp{\QQ \rr_i \times \nn} } = -\frac{d}{dt} \sum_i m_i \vv_i \cdot \gp{\rr_i \times \nn} \\ &= \nn \cdot \frac{d}{dt} \sum_i \rr_i \times m_i \vv_i \end{align} $$ This gives the angular momentum.
