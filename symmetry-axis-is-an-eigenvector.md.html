Let $(\OO, \nn)$ describe a plane through point $\OO$ with normal $\nn$, $\norm{\nn}=1$. $$ \begin{align} \CC &= \rho \int_\Omega \rr \rr^T\,dV \\ &= \rho \int_{\Omega_+} \rr \rr^T\,dV + \rho \int_{\Omega_-} \rr_- \rr_-^T\,dV \\ \rr_- &= \rr - 2 (\rr^T \nn) \nn \\ \CC &= \rho \int_{\Omega_+} \rr \rr^T\,dV + \rho \int_{\Omega_+} (\rr-2(\rr^T\nn)\nn) (\rr-2(\rr^T\nn)\nn)^T \,dV \\ &= 2 \rho \int_{\Omega_+} \rr \rr^T\,dV + 4 \rho \nn \nn^T \int_{\Omega_+} (\rr^T\nn)^2 \,dV - 2 \rho \int_{\Omega_+} (\rr^T\nn) \rr \nn^T\,dV - 2 \rho \int_{\Omega_+} (\rr^T\nn) \nn \rr^T\,dV \end{align} $$ Let $\uu \cdot \nn = 0$, $\norm{\uu}=1$, and $\rr_\parallel = \rr - (\rr^T\nn)\nn$. Then $\rr_\parallel = \beta \uu$. $$ \begin{align} \CC \uu &= 2 \rho \int_{\Omega_+} \rr \rr^T \uu \,dV - 2 \rho \int_{\Omega_+} (\rr^T\nn) \nn \rr^T \uu \,dV \\ &= 2 \rho \int_{\Omega_+} \rr^T \uu \gp{\rr - (\rr^T\nn)\nn} \,dV \\ &= 2 \rho \int_{\Omega_+} \rr^T \uu \rr_\parallel \,dV \\ &= 2 \rho \beta (\int_{\Omega_+} \rr^T \uu \,dV) \uu \end{align} $$ It is an eigenvector of $\CC$ (and also inertia $\II$).
